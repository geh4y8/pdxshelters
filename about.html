We are a team of web developers, data scientists, researchers, and educators passionate about solving the problems homeless people face in Portland, Oregon.

Our mission is three-fold, we are using technology to:

  • Connect people both inside and outside of shelters to resources
  • Organize a network of shelter employees, volunteers, and resource providers
  • Heighten public consciousness about this escalating problem

How are we doing this?

We are creating an interactive, user-friendly, and open-source web platform, fit for use on mobile.

Features include:

  • Real-time shelter bed availabilities, in addition to a comprehensive list of Portlandâ€™s shelter, food, clothing and medical resources, viewable as a map centered on the users current location.
  • Allow shelters at capacity to check neighboring shelters simultaneously for backup relief, as well as send out push notifications to an extended family of aid workers and volunteers. A live feed on the app will display resource updates and requests.
  • Streamline the intake processes at understaffed shelters, so providers can spend less time checking guests in and more time developing caring, trusting relationships.
  • Track resource requests and deliveries, providing a broader statistical picture of homelessness in the Portland metro area. We know far more about the people inside shelters than we do about those left out of them. Helping those on the fringes will provide a more comprehensive understanding of homelessness in our community.

There is a lot of work to be done so let us know if you can help!

We are creating a nexus of communication shared by shelters, aid workers, volunteers, and people who need relief. When people know how many opportunities there are to give and receive, it will in turn inspire them to help and ask for help.
